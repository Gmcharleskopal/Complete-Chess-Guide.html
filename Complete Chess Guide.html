How to Set Up a Chess Board

Make sure the bottom-right square is a light square, same as the top left square.

Pawns

Fill the entire second rank.

Rooks

Placed on the corners of the board.

Knights

Next to the rooks.

Bishops

Beside the knights.

Queen

Goes on her own square color.

King

Placed next to the queen.

Understand How Each Chess Piece Moves

Pawn

Moves forward one square (two on first move). Captures diagonally.

Knight

Moves in an “L” shape and can jump over other pieces.

Bishop

Moves diagonally.

Rook

Moves vertically or horizontally.

Queen

Can move horizontally, vertically, or diagonally,the most powerful piece like a rook and bishop together!

King

Moves one square in any direction. Keep it protected!

Special Chess Rules

  • Castling: Move the king two squares toward a rook, then move that rook next to the king.

  • En Passant: A pawn capturing move that happens immediately after a pawn advances two squares.

  • Pawn Promotion: When a pawn reaches the far end, it becomes a queen, rook, bishop, or knight.

  • Check & Checkmate: Check means your king is under attack; checkmate means the game is over.

Basic Chess Principles You Must Know

  • Control the center early with your pawns.

  • Develop your pieces before moving the same one twice.

  • Castle early to protect your king.

  • Always check what your opponent is planning.

  • Think twice before you move — one blunder can end the game.

Famous Chess Openings

Here are the few openings that i love using:

Italian Game: 1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.Bc4 - Focuses on fast development and center control.

King’s Gambit: 1.e4 e5 2.f4 - A daring opening offering a pawn for quick attack chances.

Sicilian Defense: 1.e4 c5 - A powerful counterattack setup for Black.

Queen’s Gambit: 1.d4 d5 2.c4 - White offers a pawn to gain control of the center.

Understanding Chess Notation

Chess notation is a simple way to record and communicate chess moves. It helps you study games and share strategies with others.

Files (columns): labeled a to h from left to right.

Ranks (rows): numbered 1 to 8 from bottom (White’s side) to top.

Each square is named by combining its file and rank

Examples of Moves

  • e4 : Pawn moves to e4.

  • Nf3 : Knight moves to f3.

  • Bc4 : Bishop moves to c4.

  • O-O : Kingside castling.

  • O-O-O : Queenside castling.

  • Qxd5 : Queen captures a piece on d5.

  • e8=Q : Pawn promotes to a Queen on e8.

  • + : Check

  • # : Checkmate.
